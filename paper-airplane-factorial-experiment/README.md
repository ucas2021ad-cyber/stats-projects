# ✈️ Paper Airplane Factorial Experiment

## 📌 Overview

This project applies factorial experimental design and ANOVA to analyze how design choices affect paper airplane flight distance.

The goal is to understand how different factors contribute to performance using a structured statistical experiment.

---

## 🔍 Research Question

How do paper type and wing shape affect the flight distance of a paper airplane?

---

## 🧪 Experimental Design

* Design: **2×2 full factorial design**
* Factors:

  * Paper Type: Thin vs Thick
  * Wing Shape: Narrow vs Wide
* Response Variable:

  * Flight distance (cm)
* Total runs:

  * 4 treatment combinations × 5 replicates = 20 observations

---

## ⚙️ Methodology

* Randomized experimental runs
* Controlled environment (indoor testing)
* Conducted:

  * Treatment mean comparison
  * Factorial effect estimation
  * Two-way ANOVA
  * Interaction analysis
  * Residual diagnostics

---

## 📈 Key Findings

* **Wing shape** → strongest effect on distance
* **Paper type** → significant positive effect
* **Interaction effect** → negligible

👉 Indicates both factors independently improve flight performance.

---

## 📊 Statistical Results

* ANOVA shows:

  * Wing shape: highly significant (p < 0.001)
  * Paper type: significant (p < 0.01)
  * Interaction: not significant

---

## 📉 Limitations

* Small sample size (20 observations)
* Manual execution introduces variability (throwing force, angle)
* Simplified experimental setting

---

## 🛠 Tools Used

* R (ANOVA, statistical analysis)
* Experimental design techniques

---

## 🎯 Takeaway

This project demonstrates how factorial design and ANOVA can be used to efficiently identify key factors and interactions in a controlled experiment.

It highlights the importance of randomization, replication, and statistical testing in drawing reliable conclusions.
